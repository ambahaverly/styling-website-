The Beginning

A Passion Born
at Eighteen

My journey into styling began at just 18, when I took my first role as a stylist's assistant and instantly fell in love — not with fashion as a concept, but with the deeply personal work of transforming how someone sees themselves.

Working closely with leading brands and collaborating with designers, I developed an obsession with the intersection of colour, form and identity. That early experience ignited a passion that led me to enrol in the prestigious Condé Nast Vogue Fashion Styling intensive — an eight-week course where I trained in both commercial and editorial styling techniques.

After years assisting a leading London personal stylist and cultivating relationships across fashion weeks in Europe, luxury styling lounges in London and the UAE, I knew it was time to build something entirely my own.

My Approach

Style is How You
Brand Yourself

I have always believed that fashion is not about following trends — it is about understanding yourself. Your colour season, your body type, your lifestyle, your ambitions. When these things align in what you wear, something remarkable happens. You stop getting dressed and start showing up.

"Knowing your colour palette is the key to understanding your style identity. It changes everything."

I specialise in modest fashion and colour and image consultancy, because I believe every client deserves to be dressed in a way that feels true to them — not to a trend cycle. Whether we are editing your wardrobe, shopping together at Harvey Nichols, or connecting online from across the world, my approach is always the same: deeply personal, deeply considered, and entirely focused on you.

The Person
Behind the Styling

The Writer

Long before I was a stylist, I was a writer — or at least, I dreamt of being one. Fashion gives me endless things to say, and one day you might just find me with a column or a podcast. The obsession with clothes and culture never really switches off.

✈︎

The Traveller

I am endlessly grateful that travel has become a genuine part of my work — from fashion weeks across Europe to styling sessions in the UAE. Every city teaches me something new about how people dress, present themselves and express who they are.

◈

The Obsessive

Colour theory, body analysis, the relationship between what we wear and how we feel — these are not just my work, they are a genuine obsession. I am always learning, always studying, always asking why certain things make people glow and others simply do not.

In The Industry

From Fashion Weeks
to Front Rows

My career has taken me to fashion weeks across Europe, into the workrooms of designers, and onto the sets of editorial shoots for musical artists and fashion creatives. These experiences are not decorative — they directly inform the quality of work I bring to every single client session.

Having styled for artists including Sophie Castillo and worked with editorial clients such as Maddie Lainchbury, I understand both the high-pressure world of creative production and the deeply personal work of one-to-one styling.
